Brief Bio

Since 2018, I have been working at PAII Inc, which is directed by Dr. Le Lu. My primary role is as a team lead for projects related to the radiotherapy target contouring (GTV/CTV/OAR/LN). I work with extraordinarily talented colleagues and physicians! Our team has developed the state-of-the-art segmentation and parsing models for esophageal gross tumor volume (GTV), clinical target volume (CTV), and > 70 comprehensive head & neck & chest organs at risk (OARs). These models are currently under two large-scale multi-center clinical evaluation studies, and methods have been presented in MICCAI 2019 (invited to MedIA MICCAI-2019 selected papers special issue) and CVPR 2020. We are also working on the challenging metastasis lymph node detection problem. Some initial works have been presented in MICCAI 2020. The 12 thoracic lymph node station parsing work has been accepted in MICCAI 2021.


Before that, I was a visiting research fellow at National Institutes of Health (NIH) worked on pulmonary image analysis, including the lung, lobe and airway tree segmentation and the interstitial lung disease detection. I received my Ph.D. at University of Iowa in 2016 under the supervision of Prof. Punam Saha and Prof. Eric Hoffman. In my Ph.D., I developed two 3D skeletonization (surface and curve) algorithms that can accurately and robustly extract the medial surfaces or centerlines of the 3D structures while keeping their topology. These algorithms have also been successfully applied in the topological and geometrical quantifications of trabecular bones and tubular anatomies, such as airway and coronary artery.
